Home Projects

One project that has been meaningful to me is volunteering at the Engineers Without Borders at Northeastern University. EWB is an organization that works with communities around the world to design and implement sustainable engineering solutions. Our chapter works on projects that improve infrastructure, access to clean water, and overall quality of life in partner communities. We work in two locations Panama and Uganda.

Because I am involved in this club, I have had the opportunity to work on projects that combine technical work with community communication. One of the projects our team works on involves improving water access for a community in Panama. The process involves looking up the local environment, designing sustainible systems, and working closely with community members to confirm their needs. When working in projects like this it requires strong teamwork, planning, and communication because the work involves many people with different backgrounds and perspectives.

Being part of EWB has helped me understand how engineering and problem solving can be used to make a real impact.It has also helped me understand the importance of communication and understanding the people you are working with. These experiences have been some of the most rewarding parts of my time at Northeastern because they allow students to apply what they learn in meaningful ways.

Skills Developed Through EWB

  • Community-centered design
  • Project planning and coordination
  • Technical research and data collection
  • Team collaboration and leadership
  • Sustainable engineering thinking
  • Cross-cultural communication
