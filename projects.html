One project that has been especially meaningful to me is my work with Engineers Without Borders (EWB) at Northeastern University. EWB is a student organization that works with communities around the world to design and implement sustainable engineering solutions. Our chapter focuses on projects that improve infrastructure, access to clean water, and overall quality of life in partner communities.

Through my involvement with EWB, I have had the opportunity to work on projects that combine technical problem solving with community collaboration. One of the projects our team works on involves improving water access for a community in Panama. The process involves researching the local environment, designing systems that are sustainable, and working closely with community members to ensure the solutions meet their needs. Projects like this require strong teamwork, planning, and communication because the work involves many people with different backgrounds and perspectives.

Being part of EWB has helped me understand how engineering and problem solving can be used to make a real impact. It has also helped me develop practical skills related to teamwork, project management, and working with communities. These experiences have been some of the most rewarding parts of my time at Northeastern because they allow students to apply what they learn in meaningful ways.

Skills Developed Through EWB

  • Community-centered design
  • Project planning and coordination
  • Technical research and data collection
  • Team collaboration and leadership
  • Sustainable engineering thinking
  • Cross-cultural communication
